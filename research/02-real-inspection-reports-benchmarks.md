# European Bridge Inspection Reports — Real-World Benchmarks

## 1. Publicly Available European Bridge Inspection Reports & Standards

### UK — National Highways

**Standards:** CS 450 (formerly BD 63) — "Inspection of highway structures" (DMRB). Three types:
- **General Inspection (GI)** — visual, every 2 years
- **Principal Inspection (PI)** — close-proximity (within arm's reach), every 6 years
- **Special Inspection (SI)** — triggered by specific concerns

**Condition Rating — Bridge Condition Indicator (BCI):**
- Scale: 0–100 (BCIav = average, BCIcrit = worst element)
- Categories: Very Good (>=90), Good (>=80), Fair (>=65), Poor (>=40), Very Poor (<40)
- Individual elements rated severity 1–5 + extent A–E (percentage affected)

**Statistics (2023):**
- ~8,600 structures on Strategic Road Network (SRN)
- 68% Good or Very Good, 25% Fair, 7% Poor or Very Poor
- ~3,000+ local authority bridges with weight restrictions or substandard (RAC Foundation)
- Local authority maintenance backlog: £5.6 billion (ADEPT 2023 estimate)

**Typical PI Report Sections:**
1. Structure reference number, road, span, construction type, year built
2. Photographs of each elevation, underside, and specific defects
3. Element-by-element condition assessment table (severity/extent codes)
4. BCI calculation summary
5. Recommended maintenance with priority 1 (urgent) to 5 (routine)
6. Estimated costs per action
7. Next inspection date

Source: https://www.racfoundation.org/research/economy/local-authority-bridges

### Germany — BASt / DIN 1076

**Standards:** DIN 1076 — "Engineering structures in connection with roads — inspection and testing"
- **Hauptprüfung (Main Inspection)** — every 6 years, close-proximity
- **Einfache Prüfung (Simple Inspection)** — at 3-year midpoint
- **Laufende Beobachtung** — continuous surveillance

**Condition Rating — Zustandsnote (1.0–4.0):**
| Grade | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1.0–1.4 | Very good (Sehr guter Zustand) |
| 1.5–1.9 | Good (Guter Zustand) |
| 2.0–2.4 | Satisfactory (Befriedigender Zustand) |
| 2.5–2.9 | Inadequate (Nicht ausreichender Zustand) |
| 3.0–3.4 | Insufficient (Ungenügender Zustand) |
| 3.5–4.0 | Inadequate structural safety (Ungenügende Standsicherheit) |

Calculated from damage ratings in three dimensions: **Standsicherheit** (structural safety), **Verkehrssicherheit** (traffic safety), **Dauerhaftigkeit** (durability).

**Brückenzustandsbericht Statistics (2023):**
- ~39,500 bridges on federal highways, ~2,100 km² deck area
- By deck area: 12% grade 1.0-1.4, 35% grade 1.5-1.9, 32% grade 2.0-2.4, 14% grade 2.5-2.9, 6% grade 3.0-3.4, 1% grade 3.5-4.0
- **42-46% of bridge deck area rated Zustandsnote 2.5+ (2024 Bundestag data, Drucksache 20/14188), with ~10-12% rated 3.0+ (insufficient or worse)**
- Annual bridge investment: ~€4.5 billion/year (from ~€1B pre-2015)
- Modernization target: replace/rehabilitate 4,000 bridges by 2030

**German Report (Prüfbericht) Content:**
1. Bauwerk identification (BASt number, road, km)
2. Construction details (type, materials, year, spans)
3. OSA damage codes (Ort/location, Schaden/damage, Auswirkung/effect)
4. Each damage rated for S (safety), V (traffic), D (durability) on 0–4
5. Photos with reference sketches showing damage locations
6. Overall Zustandsnote
7. Recommended measures with urgency

Source: https://www.bast.de/DE/Statistik/Bruecken/Brueckenstatistik.html

### France — Cerema / IQOA

**Standards:** ITSEOA — technical instruction for bridge surveillance and maintenance
- **Surveillance continue** — ongoing monitoring
- **Visite IQOA** — visual assessment every 3 years (national network)
- **Inspection détaillée** — detailed every 6–9 years
- **Inspection subaquatique** — submerged elements

**IQOA Classification:**
| Class | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Good apparent condition, routine maintenance |
| 2 | Non-structural defects, preventive maintenance needed |
| 2E | Repair can wait (entretien spécialisé) |
| 3 | Structural repair needed |
| 3U | Urgent structural repair (risk of restriction/closure) |

**Statistics (2022-2023):**
- ~12,000 bridges on national network; ~100,000+ total all networks
- National: 38% Class 1, 45% Class 2/2E, 14% Class 3, 3% Class 3U
- **17% rated Class 3/3U (structural issues)**
- June 2019 Senate report (deposited June 26, 2019, following the August 2018 Morandi collapse): ~25,000 bridges in poor condition, **7% of state-managed bridges presented long-term collapse risk (~840 on the ~12,000 non-conceded state network)**
- Repair backlog: €3–5 billion for national network alone
- Communal bridges (small municipalities) are worst maintained — many never professionally inspected

Source: https://www.senat.fr/rap/r18-609/r18-609.html

## 2. US Comparison — FHWA / NBIS

**National Bridge Inventory (NBI):**
- ~617,000 bridges (spans >20 feet on public roads)
- Publicly downloadable: https://www.fhwa.dot.gov/bridge/nbi.cfm

**NBI Condition Ratings (0–9):**
| Rating | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 9 | Excellent |
| 7 | Good |
| 5 | Fair |
| 4 | Poor (Structurally Deficient threshold) |
| 3 | Serious |
| 2 | Critical |
| 1 | Imminent Failure |
| 0 | Failed |

Three components rated: Deck (Item 58), Superstructure (Item 59), Substructure (Item 60).

**Element-Level (since 2014):** AASHTO CoRe elements in 4 condition states (CS1=Good through CS4=Severe).

**US Statistics (2023):**
- Structurally deficient: ~42,000 (6.8%)
- Fair: ~46%, Good: ~47%
- Average bridge age: 44 years
- Repair backlog: $125+ billion (ASCE estimate)

**Load Rating:** Rating Factor (RF) >= 1.0 = adequate. Methods: LFR or LRFR per AASHTO MBE.

## 4. What Professional Reports Look Like

### Good Report Characteristics:
1. Complete element coverage — every accessible element documented
2. Quantified defects — crack widths measured, spall areas in m², section loss estimated
3. Consistent photography — systematic, well-lit, with scale references
4. Trend analysis — comparison with previous inspections
5. Actionable recommendations — specific methods, costs, deferral consequences
6. Risk-based prioritization — urgency linked to structural safety
7. Material testing references — chloride profiles, concrete cores

### Bad Report Characteristics:
1. Vague descriptions — "Some cracking on soffit" without measurements
2. Missing elements — "couldn't see bearings"
3. Poor photos — blurry, no scale
4. No trend tracking — each inspection isolated
5. Copy-paste from previous report
6. Generic recommendations — "Monitor and re-inspect"
7. Inconsistent rating — same defect rated differently by different inspectors

### The #1 Problem: Inspector Variability
FHWA study (FHWA-RD-01-020) found **substantial inspector variability — routine inspections produced on average four or five different rating values for the same primary components, though 95% of ratings fell within two points of the average** (FHWA-RD-01-020).

## 5. What AI Can Uniquely Improve

1. **Consistency** — the #1 acknowledged problem. AI rates identically every time.
2. **Crack measurement** — CV achieves ±0.05mm accuracy in research settings
3. **Difficult access** — drones reduce costs 40–75% for tall structures (FHWA estimate)
4. **Continuous monitoring** — IoT/SHM between inspection cycles
5. **Change detection** — comparing successive point clouds/images automatically
6. **Report QA** — flagging incomplete inspections, inconsistent ratings
7. **Network prioritization** — optimizing across thousands of structures simultaneously
